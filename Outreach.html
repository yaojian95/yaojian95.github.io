Why sky is blue? Red sunset ? White cloud?
  • Molecules in the atmosphere scatter more blue light than red. Scattered light intensity is proportional to the fourth power of frequency.
  • At Horizon, we can only see the light without scattered, that is red.
  • When the Sun is above us, we see the more scattered blue light.
  • Our eyes are somewhat more sensitive to blue light than to violet light, so the sky appears blue.
